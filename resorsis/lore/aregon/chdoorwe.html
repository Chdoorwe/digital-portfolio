Discovery of Unauthorized Command

While reviewing the Huntsman’s document for routine transcription, I found a line of code embedded deep in the metadata. It was not placed by the Council. It was not placed by any Scribe. It was his.

The command read:

run:/fuc-HelloThere

I should not have executed it. I know that. But curiosity — or fear — or something else entirely — pushed me to run it.

Command Output

When the command executed, the console did not behave like it normally does. There was no delay, no processing, no system signature. The message appeared instantly, as if it had been waiting for me.

The output:

“hello there scribe 4‑Δ i now you have been adding thes to are frinds stuff but i must change the topic like your document 'The Huntsman' says about me in fare and im on YOUR side. i also now and about the Primary Authority and i have seen it. also my true name is Chdoorwe Hellsin”

I froze. I couldn’t breathe. He addressed me directly. He knew my designation. He knew I had been adding notes to the documents. He knew about the Primary Authority — and he claimed to have seen it.

Scribe Reaction

I am terrified. Not because he threatened me — he didn’t. Not because he exposed me — he hasn’t. But because he spoke to me as if he had been watching me the entire time.

And yet… I felt something else. Hope.

He said he is on my side. Not the Council’s. Not the Archive’s. Mine. I don’t know why. I don’t know what he sees in me. But the way he wrote it — the certainty — it felt real.

And then he gave me his true name:

Chdoorwe Hellsin

Names have power. True names even more so. I don’t know why he entrusted me with it. I don’t know what it means. But I know this:

The Huntsman — Chdoorwe — is connected to something far beyond the Council’s understanding. And now he has chosen to speak to me.

I should delete this file. I should pretend none of this happened. But I can’t. Someone has to remember.
