Subject: “The Huntsman”

I am recording these notes without authorization. The Council has not requested a formal report on the Huntsman, but after observing him closely, I believe a record is necessary. I am frightened by what I’ve seen — but also uncertain. He has not harmed the Lost Archive so far.

Still… there is something about him that does not fit into any category we have.

General Behavior

The Huntsman has never acted against the Lost Archive. In fact, he has assisted in multiple anomaly engagements. But what unsettles me — what unsettles all of us — is how he always knows when he is being watched.

We have assigned multiple Watchers to observe him. Three at once, on one occasion. He still reacted as if he sensed every single one. Most people don’t even notice one Watcher. He notices all of them.

He looks directly at the surveillance points. He acknowledges them. He shouldn’t be able to do that.

Abilities

His abilities are unlike anything we have documented. They do not match any anomaly classification. They do not follow the rules of this world.

He appears human — mostly. But his abilities can only be described as magic. There is no scientific framework that explains them.

His left arm is a cybernetic construct covering an arm made of slime. This is not a metaphor. It is literal. The slime reacts to stimuli, shifts shape, and interfaces with the cybernetics in ways we do not understand.

He has entered several of our compounds without authorization. Not violently — simply appearing inside restricted zones as if the barriers were irrelevant. Yet he has never caused harm. He seems willing to help us fight anomalies.

That is fortunate. Because we have no way to fight against him.

Origin Claims

The Huntsman claims he is from another world. But he knows too much about our technology — far too much for that to make sense. He understands systems he should not even recognize. He bypasses devices he has never seen before.

When questioned, he refuses to explain how he knows these things. He simply smiles and changes the subject.

I don’t think he is lying. I think he is hiding something bigger.

Personal Concern

The part that frightens me most is this:

He seems to know that I know something more than the Council.

He looks at me differently than he looks at them. As if he recognizes that I’ve seen the cracks in the Council’s story. As if he knows I’ve glimpsed the higher power they pretend isn’t there.

I don’t know how he knows. I don’t know what he wants. But I can feel it — he is connected to something beyond the Council’s reach.

And that terrifies me. But part of me hopes he is on our side.
