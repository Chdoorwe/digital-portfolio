Initial Entry

I should not be writing this. I know what happens to Scribes who notice patterns they aren’t meant to see. But I can’t ignore what Operative Leon Kennedy is beginning to understand.

He’s seeing the cracks. He’s seeing the lies. And he’s seeing that the Council is not the top of the chain.

I’m terrified of what this means for him. But I’m hopeful too. Someone finally sees the truth.

Behavioral Indicators

Leon has started questioning the Council’s reports. Not openly — he’s too disciplined for that — but in subtle, careful ways. He compares mission intel to what he actually encounters. He notices discrepancies others overlook. He hesitates before accepting Council explanations.

During a recent debrief, he said quietly:

“These numbers don’t add up. Someone’s hiding something.”

He wasn’t angry. He wasn’t confused. He sounded like someone who had finally realized the world is bigger — and darker — than he was told.

Evidence of Realization

I reviewed his mission logs. There are patterns — moments where Leon stops mid‑sentence, as if reconsidering what he’s been taught. Moments where he looks at Council members with suspicion instead of trust.

Extracted lines:

• “The Council’s version doesn’t match what I saw.”
• “Why would they lie about casualty numbers?”
• “There’s someone above them. I can feel it.”
• “If the Council isn’t in control… who is?”

That last line made my hands shake. He’s close to the truth — dangerously close.

Council Awareness

The Council has no idea Leon is beginning to see through them. They still believe he is their most loyal operative — obedient, unquestioning, predictable.

They don’t realize he’s starting to understand that they answer to something else. Something they refuse to acknowledge. Something they fear.

If they discover Leon’s suspicions, they will intervene. They will “correct” him.

I can’t let that happen.

Personal Reflection

I’m scared. I won’t deny it. The truth above the Council is not meant for people like me. I’ve seen fragments — enough to know it’s real, and enough to know it’s dangerous.

But Leon… Leon isn’t breaking. He’s awakening.

And for the first time in my life, I feel hope. If anyone can expose the truth — if anyone can survive knowing it — it’s him.
